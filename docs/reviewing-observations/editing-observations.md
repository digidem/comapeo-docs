You can edit an observation after it has been saved to correct details, improve clarity, or add additional information. Editing helps maintain data accuracy and ensures observations remain useful for the entire team.


This page explains which parts of an observation can be edited, how to access the editor, and how to save your changes.


---


## **Opening an Observation to Edit**


To edit an observation:

1. Open the observation from the Map or the Observations List.
2. Tap the :app-icon-edit: **Edit** button.

You will be taken to the **Edit Observation** screen, which looks similar to the screen used when creating a new observation.

## **What You Can Edit**


Some fields can be updated after an observation has been saved. Editable fields depend on your project configuration but generally include:


### **Editable Fields**

- **Description / Notes**
- **Category-specific fields** (text fields, dropdowns, checkboxes)
- **Additional photos** (you may add new photos)
- **Additional audio recordings** (if supported)

### **What Cannot Be Edited**


These values are locked once the observation is saved:

- **GPS coordinates**
- **Creation date and time**
- **Existing saved photos** (cannot be deleted; only new ones can be added)

This ensures the core evidence of the observation remains reliable.

## **Editing Notes and Details**


Most edits happen in the **Details** section.


To update text or structured fields:

1. Tap the field you want to change.
2. Enter your new information.
3. Move to the next field or close the keyboard when finished.

Use clear, descriptive language so others can understand what you observed.

## **Adding Photos to an Existing Observation**


You may add photos after an observation is saved.


To add a photo:

1. Tap :app-icon-camera: **Add Photo**.
2. Take a new picture or select one from your device.
3. The new photo appears in the Photos section.

Photos that were originally saved **cannot** be removed or deleted.

## **Adding Audio (If Supported)**


Some projects may allow audio recordings as additional evidence.


If enabled:

1. Tap :app-icon-microphone: **Record Audio**.
2. Record the audio clip.
3. Save the clip to add it to the observation.

## **Saving Your Changes**


After editing:

1. Tap :app-icon-save-observation: **Save**
2. Your changes are applied immediately
3. The app returns you to the observation review screen

Saved edits are included in future syncs, ensuring other devices receive the updated information.

## **Understanding Edit Limitations**


Some projects restrict what can be changed after creation. Examples include:

- Required fields that cannot be cleared
- Fields that only allow adding new data (e.g., new photos, but not removing old ones)
- Configurations where editing may be fully disabled once synced or shared

If you cannot change a field, it will appear disabled.


---


## **Tips & Good Practices**


:::note 💡
Use editing to add clarity, for example, specifying species names, adding contextual notes, or improving descriptions after fieldwork.


💡 Add photos soon after recording an observation to reduce the chance of forgetting important visual details.


⚠️ GPS coordinates and original photos cannot be changed. Make sure they are correct when you first save the observation.
:::
