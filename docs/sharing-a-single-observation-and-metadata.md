# **Sharing a Single Observation Metadata**


You can share the metadata from one observation—such as its details, category information, location, and timestamps—without sharing the full project or exporting everything. This is useful when you need to send information about a specific observation to a teammate, report, or communication channel.


This page explains how to access the share options for an observation, what information is included, and how to choose a sharing method.


---


## **Accessing the Share Option**

1. Open the observation you want to share from the Map or the Observations List.
2. Tap the :app-icon-share: **Share** button.
3. A sharing menu appears with options offered by your device.

## **What Metadata Gets Shared**


When sharing an observation, the app generates a text or formatted block containing:

- **Category**
- **Description / Notes**
- **Location (GPS coordinates)**
- **Date and time recorded**
- **Observation ID**
- **Any additional metadata saved with the observation**

Photos are _not_ included when sharing metadata.


To share photos, see **Exporting All Observations** or attach images manually after sharing.

## **Sharing Methods**


Depending on your device, you may share metadata through:

- Messaging apps
- Email
- Bluetooth
- Nearby sharing services
- Notes apps
- Clipboard (copy and paste)

After tapping :app-icon-share: **Share**, choose the app or method that best fits your needs.

## **Copying Metadata to Clipboard**


If you prefer to paste the metadata into a report or document:

1. Tap **Copy** (if shown in the share menu).
2. Paste the metadata into any text field or app.

This is helpful for adding observation details to chat messages, incident logs, patrol reports, or offline notes.


---


## **How the Metadata Is Formatted**


The shared metadata is structured clearly for easy reading. For example:


```plain text
Category: Animal Sighting
Description: Prints spotted along the riverbank
Location: -3.19201, -59.88271
Recorded: 14 March 2025, 09:21
Observation ID: 1234abcd
```


This ensures the receiver understands the context without needing the app.

## **Privacy & Sensitivity Notes**


:::note ⚠️
<callout icon="⚠️" color="gray_bg"> Shared metadata may contain sensitive information such as coordinates or identifying notes. Always ensure you have permission to share this data with external contacts. </callout>


💡 <callout icon="💡" color="gray_bg"> If an observation contains sensitive cultural or environmental information, consider sharing only the details necessary for the situation. </callout>
:::

## **Use Cases for Sharing Metadata**


You might use this feature when:

- Sending an urgent observation to a supervisor
- Sharing coordinates with a patrol team
- Reporting an environmental incident
- Organizing observations in an external document
- Collaborating with a researcher or partner who does not use the app

Sharing a single observation allows fast, targeted communication without exporting or syncing the entire project.
