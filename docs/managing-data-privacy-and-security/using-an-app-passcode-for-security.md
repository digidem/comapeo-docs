A **passcode** protects your project data by locking the app when you’re not using it. This prevents others from opening the app or seeing sensitive information if they borrow or access your device.


This page explains how to set a passcode, how it works, and how to change or remove it.


---


## **Opening the Security Settings**

1. Open the :app-icon-menu: **Menu**.
2. Tap **Settings**.
3. Select **App Passcode**.

Here you can enable, change, or disable the passcode.

## **Setting a New Passcode**


To add a passcode:

1. Tap **Enable Passcode**.
2. Enter a 4-digit code.
3. Re-enter the code to confirm.

After this, the app will require the passcode each time it is opened.

## **When the App Locks**


The app locks automatically when:

- You close the app
- Your device screen turns off
- You switch to another app (depending on device settings)

To unlock, enter your 4-digit passcode.

## **Changing Your Passcode**


If you want to update your code:

1. Return to **App Passcode** in Settings
2. Tap **Change Passcode**
3. Enter your current code
4. Enter a new code and confirm

Use a number that is easy for you to remember but difficult for others to guess.

## **Turning Off the Passcode**


If you no longer want a passcode:

1. Open **Settings → App Passcode**
2. Tap **Disable Passcode**
3. Enter your current code to confirm

The app will no longer require a code when opening.

## **What the Passcode Protects**


The passcode protects:

- Observations and tracks
- Project settings
- Team information
- Export and sharing options
- Any sensitive or confidential content in your app

The passcode does **not** lock your device—only the app.

## **If You Forget Your Passcode**


> ⚠️ <callout icon="⚠️" color="gray_bg"> If you forget your passcode, you must **reset the app**, which removes all local project data from your device. Make sure your project supports syncing or that you have a backup before resetting. </callout>


---


## **Tips & Good Practices**


:::note 💡
<callout icon="💡" color="gray_bg"> Choose a passcode that is easy for you to remember but not something obvious like “0000” or “1234.” </callout>


💡 <callout icon="💡" color="gray_bg"> Use a passcode when working with sensitive environmental, cultural, or community data. </callout>


⚠️ <callout icon="⚠️" color="gray_bg"> Never share your passcode with other people unless they are trusted team members. </callout>
:::
